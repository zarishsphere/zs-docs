## 1. What ZarishSphere Foundation is

The ZarishSphere Foundation is the institution that builds, maintains, and governs the ZarishSphere ecosystem — a universal, open-source, zero-cost, no-coder-friendly platform-of-platforms that:

1. **Indexes every global standard** across every domain of human civilization (health, education, labor, trade, environment, technology, finance, construction, human rights, humanitarian response, governance, science, culture, and all others) into a single machine-readable unified index
2. **Transforms them** into structured, deployable digital assets (forms, workflows, apps, services, decision rules)
3. **Makes them executable** through a modular platform that anyone can deploy — from air-gapped single-device to global SaaS — at zero cost
4. **Provides the complete ecosystem** — console, marketplace, builder, apps, SDK, CLI, and services — so anyone with a browser can access, customize, and deploy the world's standards without writing code

The Foundation's primary output is not software. It is a **living operating system for human civilization**: a continuously updated, openly governed, globally deployable, fully no-code ecosystem that converts every meaningful standard, treaty, guideline, specification, protocol, regulation, and framework into executable digital assets that any person, organization, or government can actually use — regardless of technical skill, budget, or connectivity.

The Foundation's governing principle is that the world's standards — the rules that govern how societies deliver health, education, justice, safety, and dignity — are not products to be sold. They are infrastructure. Infrastructure is public by default, permanently free, and designed to outlast the institution that built it.

### 1.2 What the Foundation is not

The Foundation is not a consulting firm, a software vendor, a hosted-service provider, or a data broker. It does not charge for platform access, standard entries, documentation, deployment templates, or any capability in the core ecosystem. It does not own any deployer's data. It does not hold exclusive rights over any deployment geography, sector, or use case.

## 2. The problem this institution exists to solve

The world has more authoritative knowledge — more standards, guidelines, treaties, protocols, specifications, and frameworks — governing how to deliver health, education, justice, safety, labor rights, environmental protection, trade, finance, construction, technology, and every other dimension of human civilization than at any point in history. That knowledge lives in WHO clinical guidelines, SPHERE humanitarian standards, ILO conventions, UNHCR protection frameworks, ISO technical specifications, IETF protocols, W3C web standards, IFRS accounting rules, UN human rights treaties, ICC building codes, and hundreds of thousands of equivalent documents across every domain.

It does not reach the engineer in a remote community who needs a building safety code. It does not reach the teacher who needs an educational framework. It does not reach the health worker in a refugee settlement at 11pm with inconsistent internet. It does not reach the logistics coordinator managing the last supply mile in a conflict zone. It does not reach the accountant auditing a humanitarian program against international standards. It does not reach the human rights defender documenting violations against a treaty framework. It does not reach the small business owner who needs to comply with trade regulations.

The gap between every standard and every person who needs it is not a knowledge problem. It is a systems problem.

| The standard exists as… | The worker needs… |
|---|---|
| A PDF guideline | An executable digital form |
| A treaty obligation | A decision rule in a case management system |
| A framework recommendation | A field protocol with step-by-step instructions |
| A classification system | A dropdown in a data collection tool |
| An indicator definition | A dashboard metric with a data pipeline |

ZarishSphere exists to close this distance — permanently, for free, at global scale. The Guideline-to-Action (G2A) Engine is the mechanism. The three projects (ZarishSphere Platform, Zarish Index, Zarish Standards) are the components.

## 3. The full ecosystem vision

ZarishSphere is designed to become the largest open ecosystem in human history — not through size alone, but through structural architecture. It is a total composition of every applicable "as-code" and "as-a-service" layer, built entirely with free tools, designed for no-coder access at every level.

### 3.1 Vision statement

A single, open, zero-cost ecosystem where:

- Every global standard, treaty, guideline, protocol, specification, and regulation across every domain of human civilization is indexed, machine-readable, and queryable
- Every indexed standard is automatically transformable into executable digital assets — apps, forms, workflows, services, decision rules — through a no-code pipeline
- Every person, organization, and government can access, customize, combine, and deploy these assets through a browser-based console — no coding, no terminal, no budget required
- A marketplace exists where modules, apps, templates, and standards can be discovered, combined, and deployed in one click
- Pre-built, pre-configured, pre-tested, ready-to-deploy templates exist for every scale — from a single Raspberry Pi in an air-gapped camp to a national cloud infrastructure
- The entire ecosystem is built, maintained, and governed through GitHub — every decision is a commit, every policy is a markdown file, every deployment is a workflow

|---|---|---|
| Standards | ZARISH-INDEX, ZARISH-STANDARDS | Browse, search, discover via Console |

### 3.3 Scale ambition

ZarishSphere is designed to become the unified operating system for how standards become action — across every domain, every country, every language, every deployment context. The architecture is built for:

- **100,000+ indexed standards** across 100+ domains
- **Millions of users** across thousands of deploying organizations
- **Every deployment plane** from air-gapped single device to global multi-region cloud
- **Every screen** — desktop, tablet, phone, low-bandwidth, offline
- **Every language** — full internationalization from day one
- **Zero cost at every scale** — no per-user fees, no per-deployment fees, no enterprise tier

This is not a startup. This is a new kind of infrastructure for civilization, built on a model that has never been executed before: total scope, total openness, total zero cost, and total no-coder accessibility — built by a single founder with zero budget using only free tools.

## 5. The ecosystem components

This section describes every component of the ZarishSphere ecosystem. Each component is documented before it is built (Law 2), has its own specification in `zs-docs`, and is independently deployable (Law 7).

### 5.1 ZarishSphere console

**What it is:** The browser-based control center for the entire ecosystem. Every function, configuration, deployment, and management task is achievable through the Console — no terminal access, no code editor, no programming knowledge required.

**What it does:**
- Browse and search ZARISH-INDEX across all domains
- Discover and deploy components from the Marketplace
- Configure and customize apps, forms, and workflows via GUI
- Manage deployments across planes (local, cloud, hybrid)
- Monitor system health, usage, and audit logs
- Manage users, roles, and permissions
- Access and manage your data exports
- Configure integrations between components

**No-code principle:** The Console is the primary interface for the entire ecosystem. Everything that can be done through the Console is the canonical implementation (Law 6).

### 5.2 ZarishSphere marketplace

**What it is:** A browser-based discovery and deployment hub where all ecosystem components are listed, described, rated, and installable in one click.

**What it contains:**
- Domain modules (zs-health, zs-education, zs-logistics, etc.)
- Pre-built apps (patient registry, supply chain tracker, case management, etc.)
- Form templates (intake forms, assessment tools, survey instruments)
- Workflow templates (approval chains, referral pathways, notification rules)
- Report templates (donor reports, compliance reports, dashboards)
- Standard definitions (from ZARISH-STANDARDS)
- Deployment distributions (pre-configured bundles for specific use cases)

**Licensing:** All Marketplace items are open-source (Apache 2.0 or CC BY 4.0). No paid listings. No premium tier.

### 5.3 ZarishSphere builder

**What it is:** A GUI-based creation tool that lets anyone build forms, workflows, modules, and apps without writing code.

**What it enables:**
- Drag-and-drop form builder (powered by ZARISH-STANDARDS form definitions)
- Visual workflow designer (approval flows, data pipelines, notification rules)
- Module composer (combine existing components into new configurations)
- App assembler (create custom domain applications from available modules)
- Template creator (save configurations as reusable templates for the Marketplace)

**Output:** Everything built in the Builder can be exported as code (YAML, JSON, markdown) and committed to a GitHub repository — respecting Law 1 (GitHub is the government).

### 5.4 ZarishSphere apps

**What they are:** Pre-built, ready-to-use domain applications that solve real-world problems — immediately deployable without configuration.

| Domain | Example apps |
|---|---|
| Health | Patient registry, NCD tracker, immunization scheduler, disease surveillance |
| Education | Student registry, attendance tracker, learning outcome assessment |
| Logistics | Supply chain tracker, inventory manager, distribution planner |
| Protection | Case management, referral tracker, incident reporter |
| WASH | Water point mapper, sanitation tracker, hygiene promotion |
| Nutrition | Nutritional screening, supplementation tracker, MUAC monitor |
| Human rights | Violation documentation, legal aid tracker, remedy follow-up |
| Environment | Emissions inventory, compliance tracker, impact assessment |
| (All domains) | Additional apps created via the Builder and shared via Marketplace |

### 5.5 ZarishSphere forms

**What it is:** A dynamic form engine that generates browser-based forms directly from ZARISH-STANDARDS definitions.

**How it works:**
1. A standard is indexed in ZARISH-INDEX (e.g., WHO nutrition protocol)
2. ZARISH-STANDARDS transforms it into a form definition
3. The Form Engine renders it as a browser-based form
4. Data collected populates the appropriate module
5. Forms can be customized via the Builder without code
6. Forms work offline (Plane 0-compatible)

### 5.6 ZarishSphere SDK

**What it is:** Software development kits for developers who want to build custom integrations, modules, or applications on the ZarishSphere Platform.

**Features:**
- Language-specific libraries (Go, JavaScript, Python)
- API client libraries for all ecosystem services
- Module development templates
- CLI tools (always secondary to GUI)
- Integration examples and tutorials

**Constraint:** The SDK is optional. No ecosystem function requires SDK usage. Everything achievable through the SDK is also achievable through the Console.

### 5.7 ZarishSphere CLI

**What it is:** A command-line interface for terminal-based ecosystem management. Always secondary to the Console (Law 6).

**When to use:**
- Automated deployment scripts in CI/CD pipelines
- Bulk operations (import/export, batch updates)
- Server-side configuration in headless environments
- Integration with existing DevOps workflows

### 5.8 ZarishSphere API

**What it is:** RESTful and GraphQL APIs for every ecosystem component.

**Features:**
- API-first design for all core services
- OpenAPI 3.1 specifications for every endpoint
- Authentication via GitHub OAuth or API tokens
- Rate-limited but free — no paid API tiers
- Webhook support for event-driven integration

### 5.9 ZarishSphere modules

**What they are:** Independently deployable domain packages. Each module corresponds to one domain (health, education, logistics, etc.) and contains all the forms, workflows, data models, and services needed for that domain.

**Principles:**
- Every module is independently deployable (Law 7)
- No module requires another module to function
- Modules communicate via standard APIs, not shared databases
- Modules can be combined, but never coupled

### 5.10 ZarishSphere services

## 6. The G2A engine

The Guideline-to-Action Engine is the primary technical innovation of the ZarishSphere ecosystem.

**Plain language:** Give it any indexed standard; it produces a deployable digital tool.  
**Technical:** An open-source automation system that ingests a ZARISH-INDEX entry (via ZI-UID citation), processes it through ZARISH-STANDARDS transformation rules, and outputs a deployable digital asset — a KoboToolbox XLSForm, a FHIR Questionnaire, a DHIS2 indicator definition, an SOP, or an equivalent artifact — ready for field deployment.

The G2A Engine is the mechanism through which Law 3 of the constitution (Guideline-as-Code) is operationalised. It is not a platform feature. It is the foundational operating principle from which every other platform capability derives its legitimacy.

| Input | Processing | Output |
|---|---|---|
| ZI-UID citation (`ZI-HEALTH-00001`) | ZARISH-STANDARDS transformation rules | XLSForm / FHIR Questionnaire / DHIS2 indicator / SOP |
| Standard version | Version-controlled asset | Pinned, auditable, replaceable |
| Human-readable guideline | Machine-executable rule | Deployable at Plane 0–4 |

## 7. Deployment model

ZarishSphere deploys across five named infrastructure planes. No plane is first-class. All are equal citizens of the architecture. Every ecosystem component — Console, Marketplace, Builder, Apps, Forms, every module — must function across all five planes, from fully offline to fully cloud-native. The person with the least connectivity, the least technical skill, and the fewest resources is the primary design context.

| Plane | Environment | XaaS equivalent | What the deployer owns |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 — Air-gapped | No internet. Single device. | On-premises | Everything |
| 1 — Raspberry Pi | Local network only. LAN. | IaaS + self-managed | OS + apps + data |
| 2 — District server | Server with intermittent connectivity | PaaS-like | Apps + data |
| 3 — National cloud | Self-hosted cloud. Persistent connectivity. | SaaS + config | Config + data |
| 4 — Global SaaS | Fully managed. Multi-region. High availability. | Full SaaS | Data only |

> **Constraint:** A feature that cannot function on Plane 0 — air-gapped, 4 GB RAM, no persistent internet — without modification to its core logic is not a ZarishSphere feature. See Law 4 in `001-zarishsphere-constitution.md`.

The Plane 0 compliance requirement drives the technology stack: Go-native binaries, no JVM dependencies, no cloud-native-only tooling, no runtimes with idle memory footprints above 500 MB.

## 8. Governance structure

### 8.1 Current structure

The Foundation is currently a single-founder institution. All governance, decisions, documentation, and ecosystem outputs are recorded in the `zarishsphere` GitHub organisation under the founder's sole stewardship.

| Role | Person | Contact |
|---|---|---|
| Founder and sole decision-maker | Mohammad Ariful Islam | zarishsphere@gmail.com |

All governance actions take place exclusively as pull requests, issues, or commits in public repositories under the `zarishsphere` org. There is no off-chain governance. Verbal decisions, email agreements, and private arrangements carry no standing until committed to a public repository.

### 8.2 Future governance expansion

As the ecosystem grows, the governance structure may expand to include:

No decision affecting the ecosystem's architecture, dependencies, components, or governance structure may be implemented without a prior ADR on record.

## 9. Foundation mandate

### 9.1 What the Foundation does

- Maintains all canonical repositories under the `zarishsphere` GitHub organisation
- Maintains and enforces the constitution, ZUSS standard, and governance framework
- Maintains ZARISH-INDEX as a continuously updated unified index across all domains
- Maintains ZARISH-STANDARDS as the transformation layer from index to executable assets
- Maintains the ZarishSphere Platform, Console, Marketplace, Builder, and all ecosystem components
- Publishes and maintains Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) for all significant decisions
- Ensures Plane 0 compliance for every ecosystem component
- Ensures no-coder accessibility for every ecosystem function
- Represents the ecosystem in partnerships and collaborations
- Maintains the `zs-docs` master index as the single source of truth

### 9.2 What the Foundation does not do

- Commercialise any ZarishSphere component, dataset, or service
- Create a premium tier, paid plan, or feature gate for any ecosystem capability
- Own, retain, or claim any deployer's data under any circumstance
- Grant exclusive rights to any deployment context, geography, sector, or use case
- Make governance decisions outside of public GitHub repositories
- Accept funding that creates obligations to restrict access, add proprietary features, or remove capabilities from the open-source ecosystem
